About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Electrical Engineering at the University of Notre Dame. My research focuses on measurement-driven and machine-learning approaches for wireless spectrum sharing and coexistence, combining large-scale wireless measurements with ns-3 simulations. My work includes GNSS-based environment sensing, indoor/outdoor and containment classification, Wi-Fi 6E (6 GHz LPI/SP) coexistence modeling, and cellular (NR-U) interference analysis. I am interested in developing context-aware and regulation-informed simulation frameworks to address real-world connectivity and spectrum sharing challenges.
